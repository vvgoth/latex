\section{Introduction}

\hspace*{0.5cm}This report presents the rubble mound breakwater (RMBW) design for a site located on the eastern Black Sea coast of Turkey, near Iyidere/Rize, at coordinates 41.001544°N, 40.335365°E. The site is exposed to storm waves propagating from the open northern sector of the Black Sea.

The objective of this study is to perform a complete breakwater design for two critical cross-sections, Section A-A at the breakwater head (h = 23 m) and Section D-D along the trunk (h = 22 m). The work covers four main steps, namely extreme wave statistical analysis using ERA5 reanalysis wind data and JONSWAP-based wave generation, wave transformation using the SWANONE numerical model, determination of the design water level, and armor unit sizing and cross-section design for both natural quarrystone and X-Bloc concrete armor units. 

% -------------------- Methodology --------------------
\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Extreme Wave Statistics}
\hspace{0.5cm} The extreme wave statistics were obtained in the first part of this study using hourly ERA5 reanalysis wind data extracted for a single point at 41.00°N, 40.25°E. Individual storm events were identified by applying a wind speed threshold of 3.0 m/s, and for each storm the significant wave height and peak period were estimated using the JONSWAP fetch-limited wave generation formulas. Annual maximum significant wave heights were then subjected to extreme value analysis using ten candidate probability distributions, with the best-fit distribution selected based on a scoring system. The governing 100-year return period outputs carried forward into this study are summarized in Tables \ref{tab:wind} and \ref{tab:rank}.


\subsection{Transformation of the Design Wave}
\hspace{0.5cm} Nearshore wave transformation was carried out using the SWANONE numerical model, which propagates the deep water design wave along a one-dimensional cross-shore depth profile. The bathymetric profile was constructed by first determining the deep water limit using the linear wave theory dispersion relation, and then measuring the average foreshore slope between this limit and the breakwater tip using Google Earth. The depth profile was then generated at 1 m cross-shore intervals by incrementing the depth linearly according to this average slope. The 100-year return period deep water wave conditions obtained from the extreme wave analysis were used as input, and the wave parameters at the structure toe were extracted at the corresponding depths of Section A-A and Section D-D separately.
