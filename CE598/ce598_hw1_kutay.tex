% -------------------- Introduction --------------------
\section{Introduction}

\hspace*{0.5cm}The objective of this study is to estimate deep water wave characteristics using the JONSWAP hindcasting method for given wind speed, fetch length, and storm duration conditions. The analysis focuses on the determination of significant wave height and significant wave period, and the identification of the governing wave growth condition.

The JONSWAP hindcasting method is widely used in coastal engineering for estimating wave conditions based on wind parameters. It provides a framework for calculating wave characteristics under both fully developed and developing sea states, allowing for the assessment of whether the wave growth is limited by fetch or duration.

In this report, the JONSWAP hindcasting procedure is applied to three cases. For each case, the resulting significant wave height, significant wave period, sea state, and governing limitation are evaluated and reported.

% -------------------- Methodology --------------------
\section{Methodology}

\hspace*{0.5cm}The wave hindcasting calculations are carried out using the JONSWAP method, which expresses wave growth in terms of dimensionless relationships based on wind speed, fetch, and duration. The approach is formulated to account for both fetch-limited and duration-limited growth conditions.

Following the formulation given in the class note, the wind stress factor is defined as
\begin{equation}
U_A = 0.71\,U^{1.23}
\end{equation}
where \(U\) is the wind speed at 10 m elevation above the water surface.

The dimensionless parameters used in the analysis are
\begin{equation}
F^*=\frac{gF}{U_A^2},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
H_s^*=\frac{gH_s}{U_A^2},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
T_p^*=\frac{gT_p}{U_A},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
t^*=\frac{gt}{U_A}.
\end{equation}

Using these parameters, the JONSWAP method evaluates wave growth under both fully developed and developing sea conditions. For developing sea states, the governing limitation is determined by comparing fetch-controlled and duration-controlled growth. The final wave parameters are then obtained by applying the corresponding limiting condition and converting the dimensionless quantities back to dimensional form.

In this study, the significant wave height \(H_{s0}\), significant wave period \(T_s\), sea state condition, and governing limitation were determined for each case.

% -------------------- Conclusion and Discussion --------------------
\section{Conclusion and Discussion}

\hspace{0.5cm}The JONSWAP hindcasting method was applied to the given wind, fetch, and duration cases, and the corresponding deep water wave characteristics were estimated in terms of significant wave height and significant wave period. The results also provide a classification of the sea state and identify the governing growth limitation for each case.

The calculations show that wave development can reach the fully developed condition when fetch and duration are sufficiently large relative to the wind forcing. When one of these parameters is restrictive, the sea state remains developing and the wave growth is controlled either by fetch or by duration.

For the present cases, one fully developed and two developing sea conditions were obtained. The distinction between fetch-limited and duration-limited growth demonstrates the importance of evaluating both controls in practical hindcasting studies.

function [H_s, T_s, State, Limitation] = jonswap(fetch,U_input,t_duration)

    g = 9.81;
    fetch = fetch * 1000; % Convert km to m
    t_duration = t_duration * 3600; % Convert hours to sec
    U_A = 0.71 * U_input^1.23; % Wind stress factor

    F_star = g * fetch ./ (U_A.^2);
    t_star = g * t_duration ./ U_A;

    H_s_star_FD = 0.243;
    T_p_star_FD  = 8.13;

    H_s_FD = H_s_star_FD .* U_A.^2 / g;
    T_p_FD  = T_p_star_FD .* U_A / g; 

    % Effective fetch based on duration
    F_star_eff = (t_star/68.8).^(3/2);

    H_s_star_D = 0.0016 .* min(F_star,F_star_eff).^(1/2);
    T_p_star_D  = 0.286 .* min(F_star,F_star_eff).^(1/3);

    H_s_D = H_s_star_D .* U_A.^2 / g;
    T_p_D  = T_p_star_D .* U_A / g;

    H_s = min(H_s_FD, H_s_D);
    T_p  = min(T_p_FD, T_p_D);
    T_s = 1.05 * T_p;

    if H_s_star_FD < H_s_star_D
        State = 'Fully Developed Sea';
        Limitation = '-';
    else
        State = 'Developing Sea';
        if F_star_eff < F_star
            Limitation = 'Fetch limited';
        else
            Limitation = 'Duration limited';
        end
    end
end
